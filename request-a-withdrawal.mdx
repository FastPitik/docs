Once you have an **available** balance, you can request a withdrawal to move those funds to your bank account, GCash, Maya, or another supported receiving institution. This page walks through what you need, what the fees look like, and what happens after you submit.

## **Before you start**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Your identity is verified">
    You need a verified KYC status before you can withdraw. If you haven't done this yet, head to [Verify your identity (KYC)](/kyc-verification) — it typically takes a few minutes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="You have an available balance">
    Sales sit in _pending_ first and become _available_ on the Friday of each weekly payout cycle. The cutoff for inclusion in a given week's cycle is **Tuesday at 5:00 PM** (Philippine time) — see [Pending vs available balance](/pending-vs-available) for the full timing breakdown.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Your receiving institution is supported">
    We support most Philippine bank accounts plus GCash, Maya, and a handful of other e‑wallets. The full list appears in the withdrawal dialog when you open it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The receiving account is in your name">
    The name on the receiving account must match the legal name on your verified KYC record. Requests to accounts in a different name will be declined as part of our anti-fraud and AML checks.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## **The flow**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Withdrawals tab">
    In Fokus Studio, go to **Finances → Withdrawals**. You'll see your current available balance, any amount held in pending requests, and a table of past withdrawals with their statuses.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Request Withdrawal">
    The button is disabled if your available balance is ₱0.00 or if you haven't completed KYC.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the amount">
    Type the amount you want to withdraw, or use the **quick‑pick buttons** (25%, 50%, 75%, Max) to fill in a preset proportion of your available balance. As you type, a fee breakdown appears below the amount field showing what your receiving institution will actually credit.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose your receiving institution">
    Pick from the **Payment method** dropdown. Enter the **account name** and **account number** for that institution. Double-check these — an incorrect account number can result in a failed transfer.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add an optional note">
    The _Note_ field is for your own reference and shows up in your withdrawal history. Useful for tracking which event's earnings you're withdrawing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and confirm">
    Click **Continue** to move to the confirmation screen. Read the summary, tick _"I have double-checked everything"_, then click **Submit request**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Once you submit, the request **cannot be cancelled from Studio**. The platform team reviews each request manually; if you need to withdraw a request after submitting, email [support@fokus.ph](mailto:support@fokus.ph) before the request is approved.
</Warning>

## **Fees**

<Note>
  There's a **flat ₱10 transfer fee** per withdrawal, regardless of the amount or receiving institution. The fee is deducted from the amount you enter — your institution credits the remainder.
</Note>

| You request | Fee | Your institution receives |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ₱500.00 | ₱10.00 | ₱490.00 |
| ₱2,500.00 | ₱10.00 | ₱2,490.00 |
| ₱10,000.00 | ₱10.00 | ₱9,990.00 |

The **minimum amount** per withdrawal request is **₱10.01** — just above the transfer fee.

The **maximum amount** per withdrawal request is **₱50,000.00**. You may create multiple withdrawal requests if you hit the **₱50,000.00** limit.

## **What happens after you submit**

Your request lands in our queue with a **pending** status. The platform team reviews it manually.

### **Typical review time**

Most requests are reviewed and either approved or declined on the **same business day** — usually within a few business hours of submission. Requests submitted in the evening or over a weekend are reviewed the next business day.

Some requests may take longer. We do extra checks on first-time withdrawals, withdrawals to a receiving account that doesn't appear to match the legal name on your KYC record, and any pattern that looks out of the ordinary. These extra checks rarely add more than one business day.

### **Request statuses**

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Pending" icon="clock">
    Awaiting our manual review. No funds have moved yet — your available balance is unchanged. You'll get an email when the status updates.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Approved" icon="circle-check">
    We've approved the request and initiated the transfer through PayMongo. Your available balance decreases by the full amount immediately (including the ₱10 fee). The actual settlement time to your receiving institution depends on the bank or e‑wallet — see "Settlement timing" below.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Declined" icon="circle-x">
    The request was declined. No funds moved; your available balance is unchanged. The decline reason appears in the request row in your withdrawals table. Common reasons: KYC name doesn't match the receiving account, a temporarily-paused institution, or an account number that fails the basic format check.

    You can fix the issue and submit a new request — declined requests aren't a black mark on your account.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### **Settlement timing**

After approval, the transfer is handed off to PayMongo for disbursement. Actual time to land in your receiving account **varies by institution** — there is no single SLA we can promise:

- **Major Philippine banks** (BDO, BPI, Metrobank, etc.) typically settle within hours but can take 1–3 business days during peak periods.
- **GCash, Maya, and other e‑wallets** are usually faster — often same-day, sometimes within minutes.
- **Smaller banks or rural banks** can take longer; some require a full business day to credit.

If the receiving institution returns the funds (invalid account, closed account, account name mismatch caught by the bank), we automatically reverse the withdrawal and return the amount to your available balance. You'll see a **withdrawal reversal** entry in your transaction history. From there, you can fix the receiving details and submit a new request.

## **Tracking your requests**

All past and in-progress requests appear in the withdrawal table on the Withdrawals tab. The **Status** column shows one of:

- `pending` — awaiting our review
- `approved` — review approved; transfer is being processed by PayMongo
- `declined` — request declined (reason appears in the row)

After approval, the underlying transfer has its own state (`processing`,`completed`, or `failed`) which Studio surfaces alongside the request status.

#### Withdrawal approval

Once your withdrawal request is approved, your available balance will get deducted. You will get an email confirmation from Fokus and your banking institution will credit the transfer from Paymongo. ## **FAQ**

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How often can I withdraw?">
    As often as you want, as long as you have an available balance and have completed KYC. Each request has the ₱10 transfer fee, so most photographers batch their withdrawals weekly or monthly to save on fees.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I withdraw to someone else's account?">
    No. The receiving account name must match the legal name on your verified KYC record. Withdrawals to third-party accounts are declined as part of our AML compliance.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="My withdrawal has been 'approved' for a few days but the money hasn't arrived.">
    Settlement timing depends on your receiving institution. Major banks can occasionally take 1–3 business days; smaller institutions can take longer. If it's been more than 3 business days since approval, email [support@fokus.ph](mailto:support@fokus.ph) with the withdrawal request ID from the table — we'll trace the disbursement with PayMongo.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I cancel a pending withdrawal request?">
    Not from Studio itself. Once submitted, the request is queued for manual review. If you need to withdraw a request before review, email [support@fokus.ph](mailto:support@fokus.ph) **before** it is approved. Once approved, the transfer is in flight and can't be cancelled — but if it fails, you'll get an automatic reversal.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="My request was declined — what do I do?">
    Read the decline reason in the withdrawals table. Common fixes: correct the receiving account number, switch to an institution that matches your KYC name, or contact support if the reason isn't clear. You can submit a new request right after fixing the issue.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
