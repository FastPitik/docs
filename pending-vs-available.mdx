When a customer buys one of your photos, the money does not land in your **available** balance right away. It first appears in a separate **pending** balance and stays there until the platform releases it. This page explains why, and what the two lanes mean for your cash flow.

## **The two-lane ledger**

Fokus tracks your earnings with a two‑lane system:

| **Lane** | **What it means** |
| :-- | :-- |
| **Pending balance** | Sales that have been confirmed but not yet released |
| **Available balance** | Funds you can withdraw right now |

Both figures are visible at the top of your **Finances** page in Fokus Studio.

## **Why money goes to pending first**

Every time a customer's payment is confirmed, we record a **sale** entry in your ledger and add the amount to your pending balance. The funds sit in pending for a few reasons:

- **Refund and dispute window.** Customers can raise disputes shortly after purchase. Holding funds in pending gives us time to handle any chargebacks before money reaches your withdrawable balance.
- **Payment settlement.** Payments are processed through PayMongo and typically take a few banking days to fully settle. Pending is where we wait for that.
- **Partner commission.** If your collection has a **distribution partner**, their cut is calculated during this window.

## When does my payout become available?

Payouts run on a weekly cycle with a hard **cutoff of Tuesday at 5:00 PM**. Everything a customer pays you between one Tuesday 5:00 PM and the next is bundled into a single payout that's **available to withdraw the following Friday**.

<Info>
  All cutoff times follow Philippine timezone. A `4:59 PM` payment makes the cycle; `5:00 PM` exactly does not.
</Info>

The table below traces one full cycle — the payout releasing on **Friday, June 12**. Note that payments from the **previous** weekend still land in this payout, while payments from **this** week after the cutoff roll to the next one.

| Customer paid at | In the June 12 payout? | Available to withdraw |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Mon, Jun 1 · 2:00 PM | ⏮️ Earlier cycle | Fri, Jun 5 |
| Tue, Jun 2 · 11:00 AM | ⏮️ Earlier cycle | Fri, Jun 5 |
| Tue, Jun 2 · 4:59 PM | ⏮️ Earlier cycle | Fri, Jun 5 |
| **Tue, Jun 2 · 5:00 PM** | ✅ Cycle opens | **Fri, Jun 12** |
| Wed, Jun 3 · 9:00 AM | ✅ Included | Fri, Jun 12 |
| Sat, Jun 6 · 3:00 PM _(prev week)_ | ✅ Included | Fri, Jun 12 |
| Sun, Jun 7 · 8:00 PM _(prev week)_ | ✅ Included | Fri, Jun 12 |
| Mon, Jun 8 · 2:00 PM | ✅ Included | Fri, Jun 12 |
| Tue, Jun 9 · 11:00 AM | ✅ Included | Fri, Jun 12 |
| **Tue, Jun 9 · 4:59 PM** | ✅ Included _(just in time)_ | **Fri, Jun 12** |
| **Tue, Jun 9 · 5:00 PM** | ❌ Cycle closes | Fri, Jun 19 |
| Tue, Jun 9 · 8:00 PM | ❌ Rolls over | Fri, Jun 19 |
| Wed, Jun 10 · 9:00 AM | ❌ Rolls over | Fri, Jun 19 |
| Sat, Jun 13 · 3:00 PM _(this week)_ | ❌ Rolls over | Fri, Jun 19 |

<Warning>
  **Tuesday 5:00 PM is the line.** Payments at exactly 5:00 PM or later belong to the _next_ cycle and release the following Friday — not this one.
</Warning>

<Accordion title="The rule for any week">
  - **Cutoff** — every Tuesday at 5:00 PM.
  - **A payout includes** — everything paid from the _previous_ Tuesday 5:00 PM up to (but not including) _this_ Tuesday 5:00 PM.
  - **Release** — that payout is available to withdraw on the Friday of the same week as the cutoff.
</Accordion>

### **Why the cutoff exists**

Our payment processor runs a weekly settlement window that closes Tuesday at 5:00 PM. After the cutoff:

1. PayMongo finalises that week's batch.
2. Funds settle to our merchant bank by the end of the same week.
3. We release the settled funds to your _available_ balance on Friday.

Sales received after Tuesday 5:00 PM aren't lost — they're safely recorded in your _pending_ balance and join the next week's batch.

## **What a release does to your ledger**

When the weekly release runs:

- Your **pending balance** decreases by the released amount.
- Your **available balance** increases by the same amount.
- A `Release` entry appears in your transaction history with the date.

You can see all four balances at the top of **Finances**:

| **Card** | **What it shows** |
| :-- | :-- |
| **Pending Balance** | Money in flight, queued for the next Friday release |
| **Available for Withdrawal** | What you can request to withdraw right now |
| **Total Earnings** | Lifetime sum of all sale credits |
| **Total Withdrawn** | Lifetime payouts |
