A **Collection** is how every event lives on Fokus. One race, one wedding, one company offsite — one collection. Everything you upload, sell, and get paid for is grouped under it.

This guide walks you through creating one from scratch, end to end.

## Before you start

<Note>
  You don't need to upload photos yet. You can finish the collection setup first and add photos after it's saved or approved.
</Note>

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="A cover image" icon="image">
    JPEG, PNG, or WebP. Up to 5MB. This is the thumbnail buyers see when browsing — pick something that captures the event.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Event date and location" icon="calendar">
    The actual day of the event and the city it happened in.
  </Card>

  <Card title="A price per photo" icon="peso-sign">
    Required for marketplace collections. Whitelabel (private host) galleries don't sell through Fokus, so pricing doesn't apply.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Event link (optional)" icon="link">
    Facebook event, race website, organiser's page — helps approvers verify the event is real.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Open the New Collection form

From your **Dashboard**, click **Create New Collection** in the top right. Or from the sidebar, go to **Collections → New Collection**.

You'll land on a form titled _New Collection_.

## Pick a distribution mode

This is the most important choice on the page. It changes everything that follows — what's required, what's optional, and what buttons you'll see at the end.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Marketplace" icon="store">
    Sold on **fokus.ph**. Customers find your collection through search, browse, and selfie‑search. Needs admin approval before it goes live. Pricing and platform commission apply.

    **Choose this** when you want Fokus to bring buyers to you.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Custom (private host)" icon="globe">
    _Available on the **Whitelabel** plan._ Displayed on **your own domain** (e.g. `gallery.yourstudio.com`), set up beforehand under _Settings → Custom domains_. No admin approval needed. No checkout — the gallery is free to view.

    **Choose this** when the event was paid for separately (a wedding client, a corporate gig) and you just need to share photos with the people who hired you.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  **Custom (private host) requires a Whitelabel subscription.** If you haven't subscribed yet, the option will be locked — reach out to [support@fokus.ph](mailto:support@fokus.ph) to learn more about Whitelabel, or simply use **Marketplace** mode, which is free and available to all photographers.
</Note>

<Note>
  Not sure? Pick **Marketplace**. Most race and public‑event collections live there. You can't switch a collection's mode after it's created — if you need the other mode, create a new collection.
</Note>

## Upload a cover image

Click the dashed box in the _Cover Image_ section and choose your file. The preview appears immediately. You can change it later from the collection page.

## Fill in the details

| Field | What to put |
| --- | --- |
| **Title** | Plain and specific. _"Cebu Marathon 2026 — 21K"_ beats _"Race photos"_. Minimum 3 characters. |
| **Date** | The actual event date, not the day you uploaded. |
| **Location** | City and country at minimum. _"Manila, Philippines"_. |
| **Event Details URL** | _(Optional.)_ The Facebook event, race website, or organiser's page. |
| **Event Category** | Sports / Private / Corporate / Community / Festival / Other. |
| **Sports Subcategory** | _(Only if Sports.)_ Running, Marathon, Triathlon, Cycling, Swimming, Trail Running, Fun Run, Obstacle Course, or Other. |
| **Private Note to Approvers** | _(Optional, marketplace only.)_ Anything our review team should know. Not shown publicly. |

## Set visibility and access

These two settings live together in the _Visibility & Access_ card.

### Visibility — who can find your collection

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Public" icon="globe">
    Anyone can find and view it. Appears in marketplace search and browse. This is the default.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Unlisted" icon="eye-slash">
    Only people with the direct link can view it. Won't appear in search or browse. Useful for sharing with a specific audience.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Only me" icon="lock">
    _(Whitelabel / private‑host only.)_ Nobody can access the link, even with the URL. Useful while you're still preparing the gallery.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Who can contribute — who's allowed to upload photos

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Just Me" icon="user">
    You're the only photographer. This is the default.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Invite Only" icon="envelope">
    You invite specific photographers by email. Good for an event you're co‑shooting with a small team.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Anyone" icon="user-group">
    _(Marketplace only.)_ Any photographer can request to join. Toggle **Auto‑approve** if you want their requests to skip your review queue.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Note>
  If you're running an event with multiple shooters (a marathon with a team of photographers), pick **Invite Only** or **Anyone**. Each contributor uploads their own photos and gets paid for their own sales.
</Note>

## Set your pricing

<Note>
  The pricing section disappears for Whitelabel (private‑host) collections — those galleries aren't sold through Fokus.
</Note>

### My Price per Photo

This is the price you'd _like_ buyers to pay per photo. Think of it as a suggested price — the final price that goes live on the marketplace depends on the event category.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Running & Triathlon events" icon="person-running">
    These use **standard platform pricing** set by Fokus. Even if you enter a different number, the listed price will be aligned with the standard for that event type. This keeps pricing consistent for runners across the marketplace.
  </Card>

  <Card title="All other event categories" icon="hand-holding-dollar">
    Pricing is reviewed **case‑by‑case** during approval. The price you enter is a starting point — our team may approve it as‑is, or reach out to align on a fair number based on the event type and audience.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  Why does Fokus standardise running and triathlon pricing? These are our highest‑volume event categories — keeping prices consistent makes the marketplace feel fair and predictable for buyers, which lifts conversion for every photographer covering the event.
</Note>

As you type, the **Earnings Preview** on the right updates live:

- What you take home per photo after platform fees
- A breakdown bar showing your earnings vs. the platform fee
- Projected total if you sell 10, 50, or any custom number of photos

<Note>
  **Promo rate: 15% \+ ₱4 per photo.** Fokus is currently running a promotional commission rate of **15% \+ ₱4 fixed per photo** — applied to every collection approved during the promo. Once your collection is approved, this rate is locked in, so you'll keep earning at the promo rate even after the promotion ends. We'll let everyone know before the standard rate kicks in. For non‑sports events, the final _price_ is also locked in at approval.
</Note>

### Contributor Settings _(only if contributors are enabled)_

If you allowed contributors in the previous step, decide how _their_ photos are priced:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Standard Price" icon="tag">
    All contributing photographers use one fixed price you set.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Photographer Sets Price" icon="sliders">
    Each contributor picks their own price within a **min** and **max** range you define.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Partner Commission _(approved distribution partners only)_

If your studio is an approved distribution partner, you can also earn a commission on every contributor sale in your collection. Set your rate (% and/or fixed PHP) — the form shows the maximum you're allowed.

## Save or submit

You'll see two or three buttons at the top right, depending on the distribution mode you chose.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Save as Draft">
    Saves the collection without listing it. Good if you're still gathering details or waiting on a cover image. You can come back and finish later.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit for Approval (marketplace)">
    Sends the collection to our review team. Before it goes through, you'll pick one of two publishing modes:

    - **Automatic** — The moment we approve, your collection is live. _(Recommended.)_
    - **Manual** — We mark it approved, but it stays hidden until **you** hit publish. Good if you want to time the launch yourself.

    Approval usually takes a few hours during business days.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save & Publish (Whitelabel / private host)">
    For Whitelabel collections, you can publish immediately — no admin approval needed. You'll be asked to confirm, and once you do, the collection is live on your selected domain(s) right away.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What happens next

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Marketplace, awaiting approval" icon="clock">
    Status is **Pending Approval**. You'll get an email when we review it — usually within the same business day.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Marketplace, approved + automatic publish" icon="circle-check">
    Status moves to **Published**. Time to upload photos.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Marketplace, approved + manual publish" icon="hand">
    Status sits at **Approved** until you hit **Publish** from the collection page. Use this when you want to control the exact go‑live time.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Whitelabel (private host), published" icon="globe">
    Status is **Published**. The collection is already live on your domain.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

In every case, the next step is the same: **upload your photos**. Open the collection and start the upload — Fokus handles watermarking, face detection, and bib number reading automatically.

## Frequently asked

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I change the price later?">
    For **running and triathlon events**, pricing follows the platform standard, so adjustments aren't typically needed. For **other event categories**, you can request a price change before the first sale by messaging [support@fokus.ph](mailto:support@fokus.ph) — once buyers have paid, pricing is locked to keep things fair for everyone.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Why didn't my collection list at the price I set?">
    If your event falls under **running or triathlon**, the listed price reflects Fokus's standard pricing for that category — not the number you entered. This is intentional. For other categories, our team may have adjusted the price during review; if you weren't notified, reach out and we'll walk you through the reasoning.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I switch from Marketplace to Custom (or back) later?">
    No — distribution mode is fixed once the collection is created. If you need the other mode, create a new collection.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What if my collection is rejected?">
    We'll email you with the reason. Most rejections are for unclear titles, missing event details, or copyright concerns. Edit the collection and resubmit — you don't have to start over.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How long does approval take?">
    Usually within the same business day. If it's taking longer, message us at [support@fokus.ph](mailto:support@fokus.ph).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I add contributors after the collection is created?">
    Yes. Open the collection and head to the **Contributors** section to invite by email or change the access setting at any time.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
