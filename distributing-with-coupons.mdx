## Your built-in marketing tool

Coupons let you create your own discount codes (like `SUMMER10`) and share them however you want. They're a simple, powerful way to **drive more sales on your photos** — every code is yours, on your terms, and you stay in control of the whole promotion.

## You decide the deal

A coupon is **your** promotion. You choose how much to offer, so you decide exactly how generous to be — and you can change or switch it off any time.

<Check>
  You're protected: a coupon can never bring a photo below what you earn on it, so you'll **never lose money on a sale.** The most a code can ever do is bring that photo to your break-even — never below.
</Check>

Here's how the numbers work on a **₱125 photo** — with no code, a **10% off** code, and a **₱50 off** code:

- **Fokus commission** = (price × rate) \+ fixed fee → ₱125 × 15% \+ ₱4 = ₱22.75
- **You earn** = price − Fokus commission − coupon discount

| Line | No code | 10% off | ₱50 off |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Photo price | ₱125.00 | ₱125.00 | ₱125.00 |
| Coupon discount | — | −₱12.50 | −₱50.00 |
| Customer pays | ₱125.00 | ₱112.50 | ₱75.00 |
| Fokus commission (15% \+ ₱4) | ₱22.75 | ₱22.75 | ₱22.75 |
| **You earn** | **₱102.25** | **₱89.75** | **₱52.25** |

The discount comes off the customer's price and off your earnings — your Fokus commission doesn't change. A **percentage** scales with the photo's price; a **fixed amount** takes off the same peso value every time (and is shared across your photos if a customer buys several). More fans buying at a friendly price usually beats a few buying at full price.

<Note>
  This uses an example Fokus commission of **15% \+ ₱4** — your real rate comes from your collection's pricing, and you'll always see your exact payout when you set a price.
</Note>

<Tip>
  Think of it like a marketing spend you fully control: a little off the top to bring in orders you might not have gotten otherwise — and you set the limit, so it's never a surprise.
</Tip>

## Create a coupon

You'll find coupons in your dashboard under **Finances → Coupons**.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Coupons tab">
    Go to **Finances**, then select the **Coupons** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click “Create Coupon”">
    Pick a discount type and amount, and (optionally) set when it runs and how often it can be used.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Share your code">
    Post it to your followers, send it to your top fans — wherever your audience is. It works on your photos at checkout right away.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Track and adjust">
    Watch redemptions in the table, and **pause**, **resume**, or **retire** the code whenever you like.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Choose your discount

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Percentage off">
    Takes a percentage off each of your photos in the order — e.g. `10` for 10% off.

    Add a **maximum discount** if you want a ceiling, like "15% off, up to ₱150."
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Fixed amount off">
     Takes a flat peso amount off — e.g. `50` for ₱50 off your photos in the order.

    If the customer buys several of your photos, the amount is shared across them automatically.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Set your rules

Every control is optional — use as many or as few as you like.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Active dates" icon="calendar">
    A start and/or end time. Leave blank to let it run until you turn it off.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Total uses" icon="gauge">
    Cap how many times the code can be redeemed in total — great for "first N buyers" deals.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Uses per customer" icon="user-group">
    Limit how many times one buyer can use it, so a giveaway stays fair.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Minimum photo price" icon="coins">
    Only apply the code to photos at or above a price you choose.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Manage your coupons

From the Coupons table you can change a code's status any time:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Active" icon="circle-check">
    Live and ready to redeem (within its dates and limits).
  </Card>

  <Card title="Disabled" icon="circle-pause">
    Paused. Switch it back on whenever you want.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Archived" icon="box-archive">
    Retired for good, but kept in your records.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  A coupon's **discount type and amount are locked once it's created** so your past promotions stay accurate. Want different terms? Archive the old code and create a fresh one.
</Note>

## Good to know

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Your code, your photos">
    A coupon only ever applies to **your own** photos. If a customer's cart also has photos from another studio, your code simply leaves those untouched.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Codes are unique and not case-sensitive">
    Each code is one-of-a-kind across Fokus, and `SUMMER10` and `summer10` are treated the same — so customers can't get it wrong by typing the wrong case.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="One of your codes per order">
    A customer can use one of your codes per order (and a code from another studio for that studio's photos). Pick the single best deal to promote at a time.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Free photos aren't discounted">
    Codes apply to paid photos only — there's nothing to discount on a free one.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Refunds free up a use">
    If an order is refunded or cancelled, that redemption is returned to your code's available count automatically.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Tip>
